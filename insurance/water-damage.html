Water Damage Restoration Insurance Guide

What's Typically Covered?

Most standard homeowners policies cover sudden and accidental water damage from burst pipes, appliance overflow, and similar incidents.

Common Exclusions

Flood damage, gradual leaks, sewer backups (may require endorsement), and maintenance issues are typically not covered.

  • Gradual damage or wear and tear
  • Maintenance-related issues
  • Flood damage (requires separate flood insurance)
  • Earth movement (earthquake, landslide)
  • Intentional damage

How to File a Claim

  1. Document everything – Take photos/videos before any cleanup. Make a list of damaged items.
  2. Mitigate further damage – Make temporary repairs to prevent more damage. Keep all receipts.
  3. Contact your insurance company – File your claim immediately. Have your policy number ready.
  4. Meet with the adjuster – Point out all damage. Our team can be present to help explain the scope of work.
  5. Get repair estimates – We work with insurance companies directly and provide detailed estimates.

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can you respond to water damage emergencies?
We guarantee 30-minute response time in all service areas. Our water damage restoration team is on call 24/7, including holidays and weekends. The faster we respond, the less damage occurs to your property. Call our emergency hotline for immediate dispatch.
Does homeowners insurance cover water damage?
Most standard homeowners policies cover sudden and accidental water damage from burst pipes, appliance overflow, and storm-related flooding. However, flood damage (rising water) requires separate flood insurance. We work with all major insurance companies including State Farm, Allstate, Liberty Mutual, and USAA. We can help file your claim and document damage.
How long does water damage restoration take?
Typical residential water damage restoration takes 3-5 days depending on the extent of damage. Day 1: Water extraction and drying equipment setup. Days 2-4: Active drying and moisture monitoring. Day 5: Final inspection and equipment removal. Severe cases involving structural damage may take longer.
Can you save hardwood floors after water damage?
Often yes, if addressed quickly (within 24-48 hours). We use specialized drying systems that can save hardwood by drying from underneath. Success depends on water type (clean water vs sewage), duration of exposure, and wood type. We'll assess and provide honest recommendations.
Do you handle mold if it develops after water damage?
Yes, we provide complete mold remediation services if mold develops after water damage. We're IICRC certified in both water restoration and mold remediation. Early water extraction and proper drying prevents most mold issues.
