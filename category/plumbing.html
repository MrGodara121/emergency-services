Frequently Asked Questions

What constitutes a plumbing emergency?
Call immediately for: burst pipes (water actively spraying), sewer backups (waste water coming up drains), no water at all, gas leaks (leave house and call utility immediately), major leaks causing visible damage, or overflowing toilets that won't stop. Minor drips can wait for regular hours.
How do I shut off water in an emergency?
The main water shut-off valve is typically where the water line enters your house â€“ near the water heater, in the basement, crawlspace, or outside near the foundation. Turn clockwise to close. Every family member should know its location. We can guide you by phone if needed.
How much does emergency plumber cost?
Emergency plumbing rates: $150-300 for after-hours call-out fee, plus hourly rates $100-200. Weekend and holiday surcharges apply. We provide estimate before work begins. Regular hour service costs significantly less.
Does homeowners insurance cover plumbing emergencies?
Sudden damage from burst pipes or appliance leaks is usually covered. However, damage from slow leaks, lack of maintenance, or sewer backups may have limited coverage. We document everything thoroughly to support insurance claims.
How long do water heaters last?
Tank water heaters typically last 8-12 years. Tankless heaters last 20+ years with proper maintenance. If yours is older and having issues, replacement may be more cost-effective than repair. We help choose the right size for your home.
