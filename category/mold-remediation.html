Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if I have mold in my home?
Common signs include: musty odors you can't find the source of, visible black/green patches on walls or ceilings, recent water damage history, family members experiencing allergy symptoms only at home, peeling wallpaper, or warped walls. Professional inspection with thermal imaging and moisture meters is recommended for confirmation.
Is black mold dangerous?
Some types of black mold (Stachybotrys) can produce toxins that may cause health issues, especially for those with respiratory conditions, allergies, or weakened immune systems. Symptoms can include coughing, wheezing, eye irritation, fatigue, and headaches. Any visible mold growth should be professionally remediated.
How much does mold remediation cost?
Costs vary based on extent: Small area (under 10 sq ft): $500-1,500. Medium area (10-100 sq ft): $2,000-6,000. Whole house or severe infestation: $10,000-30,000+. Factors include location (attic, crawlspace, HVAC system), accessibility, and whether drywall removal is needed. Free inspection for accurate quote.
Does homeowners insurance cover mold removal?
Mold removal is covered only if caused by a covered peril (like sudden water damage from a burst pipe). Mold from long-term leaks, humidity, or flooding is typically not covered. We work with insurance companies to document covered cases and assist with claims.
How long does mold remediation take?
Most residential jobs take 2-5 days. Day 1: Containment setup and negative air pressure. Days 2-3: Removal of affected materials and HEPA vacuuming. Day 4: Antimicrobial treatment and cleaning. Day 5: Clearance testing and final inspection. Larger projects may take longer.
