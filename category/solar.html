Frequently Asked Questions

How much do solar panels cost?
Typical residential system (6-8 kW): $15,000-25,000 before tax credits. Federal tax credit (30%) reduces cost significantly. Local and state incentives may also be available. Free custom quote based on your home's energy usage.
How much can I save with solar?
Most homeowners save $1,000-2,000 annually on electricity bills. With net metering, you earn credits for excess power sent to the grid. Systems typically pay for themselves in 7-10 years.
Do solar panels work during cloudy days?
Yes, panels continue producing on cloudy days, though at reduced output (typically 10-25% of sunny day production). Battery backup provides power during outages and nighttime.
What is net metering?
Net metering credits you for excess solar power sent to the grid at retail rates. This maximizes your savings and can eliminate electric bills entirely depending on system size and usage.
How long do solar panels last?
Panels are typically warranted for 25-30 years and continue producing beyond that. Inverters last 10-15 years (may need replacement during system life). Regular maintenance extends system life.
