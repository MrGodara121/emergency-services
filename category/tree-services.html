Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if a tree needs to be removed?
Signs: dead or dying tree (no leaves in season), large dead branches, trunk damage or cavities, fungus at base, leaning suddenly, roots damaged by construction. Certified arborist can assess risk level.
How much does emergency tree removal cost?
Costs vary: $500-2,000 for average tree removal after storm. Factors: size (height/diameter), location (near house/power lines), accessibility, and complexity. Emergency response available 24/7. Free estimate after securing scene.
Does homeowners insurance cover tree removal?
Covers removal if tree damages an insured structure (house, garage, shed). If tree falls without causing damage, removal is typically not covered. If storm-damaged tree threatens house, may be covered. We work with insurance.
How often should trees be trimmed?
Most trees need trimming every 3-5 years. Palms need annual trimming of dead fronds. Storm-prone areas benefit from regular maintenance to remove weak branches. We assess during visit.
How long does stump grinding take?
Most residential stumps are ground in 30-60 minutes. Depth is adjustable (typically 6-12 inches below grade). We clean up wood chips – can leave for mulch or haul away.
